## Screening and Notice Requirements

- Countable months toward the ABAWD time limit cannot be assigned unless the participant has been screened and determined to have no applicable exemption.
- When an individual qualifies for more than one exemption, the applicable exemption is applied (does not need to pick "best" one — any qualifying exemption suffices).
- A **Consolidated Work Notice (DSS-8569)** must be provided to participants, along with a verbal explanation of all applicable work requirements (both general work requirements and ABAWD-specific requirements).
- This notice and verbal explanation must occur at: certification, recertification, and whenever a previously exempt household member (or new household member) becomes subject to work requirements.
- The case file must document the date the notice was given, how it was delivered, and whether it was verbally explained.

## Verification of Exemption Status (7 CFR § 273.24(l))

- **Self-attestation must be accepted** for verification of ABAWD exemptions in many cases — a caseworker cannot always demand documentary proof beyond the individual's own statement.

## Detailed Exemption Criteria

### Child Under 14 in Household
- Individual is exempt if they reside in an FNS household with responsibility for a child under age 14
- The individual does NOT need to be the child's parent
- The individual does NOT need to be legally responsible for the child
- The child can be an ineligible member of the FNS household for any reason (e.g., ineligible alien status)
- The child can be temporarily absent from the home for no longer than 30 days and the exemption still applies

### Pregnancy
- No minimum length of pregnancy required to qualify for the exemption
- Exemption is effective beginning the **month of conception**
- Includes the month of the child's birth
- Continues to apply for a period after birth (specific postpartum duration not fully captured in this excerpt — verify with caseworker for exact cutoff)

### Age-Based Exemption Details
- The ABAWD work requirement no longer applies beginning the **first day of the month** when an individual ages out of the requirement (e.g., turns 65)
- At application, the caseworker must explain all ABAWD requirements that could apply during the certification period — including situations where a household member will age into or out of requirements mid-period (e.g., a 17-year-old turning 18 during the certification period)

## Bonus Months (Regaining Eligibility)

- ABAWDs who regain eligibility (by working 80 hours in a 30-day period) but are not meeting ongoing FNS work requirements can receive **an additional 3 consecutive months** once during the 3-year (36-month) eligibility period.
- Benefit calculation for individuals regaining eligibility via bonus months: benefits are prorated from the day the 30 days of qualifying work were completed.

## Historical Context: COVID-19 Waiver Transition (Background Only)

- Prior to July 1, 2023, ABAWD time limits were waived during the COVID-19 Public Health Emergency (PHE).
- After the waiver ended (July 1, 2023), normal FNS 260 policy went back into effect. NC FAST systematically removed all previously accumulated countable months and end-dated COVID-19 work non-participation evidence as of 6/30/2023.
- This is historical/background information only — not relevant to current eligibility determinations, but included to show how time-limit "clocks" can be reset by policy changes. This is itself another real-world example of why source-authority and effective-dating matters in this domain.

## H.R. 1 Transition Details (2025-2026)

- Change Notice implementing H.R. 1 ABAWD provisions: effective December 1, 2025
- Prior policy (FNS Section 260, Change #01-2024, effective March 1, 2024) remained in effect until December 1, 2025
- To mitigate payment error risk during the transition, NCDHHS used **discretionary exemptions under 7 C.F.R. § 273.24(g)** for recipients who would otherwise have been newly screened as non-exempt ABAWDs during certification/recertification in November 2025 — effectively a temporary buffer before the new rules took full effect.
- H.R. 1 Section 10103 also changes how energy assistance payments (LIHEAA or similar) are treated for the Heating and Cooling Standard Utility Allowance (HCSUA), depending on whether the household has an elderly or disabled member. This connects to FNS 340 (Deductions) and FNS 315 (Special Budgeting Income) — sections not otherwise included in this knowledge base.

## Volunteer Work Verification (FNS 260.03 B.1.c)

- Volunteer work for legitimate public/private nonprofits, churches, religious groups, community organizations, government agencies, or court-ordered community service counts toward the 80-hour requirement.
- Counties should request written or verbal verification from the volunteer agency of hours completed.
- If verified verbally, staff must thoroughly document this in case notes (no designated form required).
