# Work Requirements for Able-Bodied Adults Without Dependents (ABAWD)

**Source:** NC DHHS
**URL:** https://www.ncdhhs.gov/divisions/child-and-family-well-being/food-and-nutrition-services-food-stamps/able-bodied-adults-without-dependents-abawds
**Document type:** Official program policy page
**Related legislation:** H.R. 1 (One Big Beautiful Bill Act), effective December 1, 2025
**Last verified:** July 2026

---

## Overview

H.R. 1 changed who needs to meet work requirements to qualify for FNS/SNAP benefits. A county's Department of Social Services screens applicants for exemptions at their next recertification.

There are two separate sets of work-related rules:
1. **General Work Registration** (ages 16-59)
2. **ABAWD Time Limits** (ages 18-64)

A person may be subject to one, both, or neither, depending on circumstances.

## Screening Rights

- **Right 1:** Caseworker must screen for exemptions at every application and recertification. No countable months can be assigned toward the time limit until screening happens.
- **Right 2:** Applicants get at least 10 days to provide verification of an exemption if requested.
- **Right 3:** Missing hours don't always count against the limit — if circumstances beyond the applicant's control prevented meeting work hours ("good cause"), that month doesn't count.
- **Right 4:** Applicants can ask their caseworker for help verifying an exemption.

## Exemptions (ABAWD Time Limit Does NOT Apply If Any Apply)

- **Age:** Under 18 or 65+
- **Child in household:** Living with a child under age 14 (does not need to be applicant's own child)
- **Pregnant:** Any stage
- **Receiving disability benefits:** SSI, SSDI, VA disability (any percentage), Workers' Comp, private disability — verification required
- **Physically or mentally unfit for employment:** Condition limits ability to work 20 hrs/week. No specific "disabled" designation required. If obvious, no documentation needed; if not obvious, may require a professional statement (doctor, nurse, social worker, counselor, therapist, addiction counselor all acceptable)
- **Chronic homelessness:** Lacking fixed/regular nighttime residence (shelters, vehicles, outdoors, etc.) — North Carolina treats this as grounds for "obviously unfit for employment" exemption even though federal law eliminated homelessness as an automatic exemption category
- **Domestic violence survivor:** Recent violence affecting ability to work
- **Caregiver:** Caring for a child under 6 OR an incapacitated person of any age (does not need to live with applicant or be in applicant's FNS household)
- **Homeschool parent:** Teaching 30+ hours/week (qualifies under general work registration exemption)
- **Age 55-64 with multiple barriers:** ALL three must be present — limited education (no diploma/GED) + no recent work history + health conditions

Multiple exemptions can apply — if one ends, another may still protect the individual.

## General Work Registration (FNS 240) — Ages 16-59

Must:
- Register for work (caseworker does this)
- Accept suitable job offers
- Not voluntarily quit a job working 30+ hrs/week (or earning $217.50+/week) without good cause
- Not voluntarily reduce hours below that threshold without good cause

Excused if:
- Working 30+ hours/week (or equivalent wages)
- Meeting TANF or unemployment work requirements
- Caring for a child under 6 or incapacitated person
- Unable to work due to physical/mental limitations
- In alcohol/drug treatment program
- Student at least half-time

## ABAWD Time Limits (FNS 260) — Ages 18-64

**Effective December 1, 2025**, subject to the 3-month time limit if ALL apply:
- Age 18-64
- No one under age 14 lives in household
- Physically and mentally able to work

**Requirement:** 80 hours/month (~20 hrs/week) of work, volunteering, or approved activities.

**Time limit:** Without meeting requirement or having exemption, benefits limited to 3 months in a 36-month period. **Current period: January 1, 2025 – December 31, 2027.**

**Important:** A person can be subject to one rule set but not the other:
- Example: Age 60-64 exempt from general work registration, but may still need to meet the 80-hour ABAWD requirement unless another exemption applies
- Example: Age 50 living with a 10-year-old must register for work but is exempt from the 3-month ABAWD time limit

## Activities That Count Toward the 80-Hour Requirement

| Activity Type | Counts? |
|---|---|
| Paid work (full-time, part-time, temp, gig work like Uber/DoorDash, self-employment) | Yes |
| Unpaid work (caregiving for children/elderly/disabled you're not legally responsible for; work in exchange for goods/services/housing) | Yes |
| Volunteer work (nonprofit, church, community org, government agency, court-ordered service) | Yes |
| More Than a Job NC (job training, GED/vocational education, supervised job search through state program) | Yes |
| Combination of any of the above | Yes |
| Job search alone | No (can count up to 25% of hours if part of a work program, not on its own) |

Must report if work hours drop below 80/month by the 10th of the following month.

## Good Cause (When Hours Can't Be Met)

If circumstances beyond the applicant's control prevent meeting 80 hours, tell the caseworker immediately — that month may not count against the 3-month limit.

**Good cause includes:**
- Illness (own or caring for sick family member)
- Household emergency (eviction, utility shutoff, natural disaster)
- Transportation problems (vehicle breakdown, no public transit, can't afford gas)
- Childcare breakdown
- Court/legal obligations
- Medical appointments
- Other circumstances a reasonable person would consider valid

Good cause must be reported right away, not saved until the next recertification. It's temporary — covers temporary situations, not permanent ones.

## After Using the 3 Months

If benefits stop after 3 months without meeting requirements, eligibility can be regained by:
1. **Working 80 hours in any 30-day period** (doesn't need to be a calendar month) — then contact caseworker to reapply
2. **Becoming exempt** (pregnancy, disability benefits, moving in with a child under 14, etc.)
3. **Waiting for the period to end** — current 36-month period ends December 31, 2027; new period starts January 1, 2028

**Bonus months:** If eligibility is regained by working 80 hours but the person then stops meeting the requirement, up to 3 additional consecutive months of benefits may be available. Can only be used once per 36-month period.

## Frequently Asked Questions (Selected)

**Veterans:** Veterans receiving VA disability compensation at ANY percentage (including 20%, 40%, etc.) are exempt from the ABAWD time limit.

**Gig workers (Uber/DoorDash):** Use app work history or earnings statements to show hours. If unclear, keep a personal log and provide a signed statement of monthly hours.

**Homelessness exemption:** Federal law eliminated automatic homelessness exemption, but NC recognizes chronic homelessness as grounds for an "obviously unfit for employment" exemption if it prevents working.

**Social Security Retirement (not disability):** Does not automatically exempt — but health conditions preventing 20 hrs/week of work may still qualify for the "unfit for employment" exemption.
