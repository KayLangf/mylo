# FNS 600 — Simplified Nutritional Assistance Program (SNAP)

**Source:** NC DHHS Policy Manual (FNS 600)
**URL:** https://policies.ncdhhs.gov/wp-content/uploads/FNS-600-Simplified-Nutritional-Assistance-Program-SNAP-10.2024.pdf
**Document type:** Official policy manual (PDF) — case processing procedures
**Effective date:** October 1, 2024 (per section revision history; some subsections have been separately updated as noted below)
**Last verified:** July 2026

> **Note:** "Simplified" SNAP (sometimes called SNAP, distinct from regular FNS in NC's naming
> convention) is a streamlined program primarily for elderly individuals, identified proactively
> through a state data match rather than requiring active application-seeking. This document
> covers the identification, application, and processing procedures specific to this pathway.

---

## Who This Applies To

Simplified SNAP applies to a subset of FNS units — specifically households the state proactively identifies as likely-eligible, primarily elderly individuals, via a data-matching process (see below). It does not apply to a Transitional FNS Unit.

## Identification Process

- **North Carolina FAST (NC FAST)** identifies potential SNAP recipients via the **North Carolina State Data Exchange (SDX) match process**.
- Identified individuals are automatically mailed a **DSS-6240 SNAP Application**, with a return envelope included.
- SNAP applications under this pathway are mailed between the **4th and 10th of each month**.
- Individuals must be **65 or older by the fourth working day of the month** in order to receive an application through this proactive process.

## Household Concept Requirements

If the FNS recipient does not meet the SNAP household concept requirements to be included in the SNAP unit, NC FAST will not include them in this streamlined pathway (they would need to be handled under regular FNS application procedures instead).

## Benefit Calculation Under Simplified SNAP

- Income and deductions are **not** considered when determining the SNAP allotment amount under this pathway.
- Only **shelter costs** are used to determine the allotment amount.

This is notably different from the standard FNS benefit calculation (see FNS 360), which is based on net income after all deductions.

## Application Processing Rules

- If a signed application is returned incomplete, enter it into NC FAST and attempt to contact the individual by telephone if contact information is available.
- If no contact can be made, send **DSS-8650** requesting the missing information.
- **Deny the application** only if separate household status cannot be determined by the 30th day.
- **Approve the application** if the only missing information is: shelter expense, date of birth, or heating source information. In this case, enter zero for shelter expense evidence pending further documentation.
- Individuals with an H or I controlled substance conviction can potentially regain eligibility — mail them a regular FNS **DSS-8207 Application for Food and Nutrition Services**; if returned, process using regular FNS guidelines (not the simplified pathway).
- Applications must be processed within **30 days** of the application date.

## Certification Periods (Cross-Reference: NC Admin Code 10A NCAC 71U .0213)

Certification periods for FNS units under simplified reporting rules generally depend on household composition:

- **FNS units with only "specified individuals" (age 60+) and no earned income:** certified for **12 months**
- **Units with an ABAWD member not meeting work requirements:** may receive a **Notice of Adverse Action (DSS-8553)** during the second month of certification, warning that benefits will terminate after month three unless the ABAWD satisfies work requirements or an exception applies. Certification may be for 6 months if the county is under an active ABAWD waiver.
- **All other units:** certified for up to **6 months**

**Mandatory mid-certification reporting for Simplified Reporting households:**
- Units including an ABAWD must report if the ABAWD's average work hours drop below 80/month (consistent with the general ABAWD reporting requirement — see `06_change_of_circumstance_reporting.md`)
