# SNAP Noncitizen Eligibility

**Source:** NC DHHS
**URL:** https://www.ncdhhs.gov/divisions/child-and-family-well-being/food-and-nutrition-services-food-stamps/snap-noncitizen-eligibility
**Document type:** Official program policy page
**Related legislation:** H.R. 1 / One Big Beautiful Bill Act (Public Law 119-21), effective February 1, 2026
**Related policy manual:** FNS 227 (Noncitizen Eligibility), Change Notice FNS-CN-01-2026
**Last verified:** July 2026

---

## Overview

Federal law (H.R. 1) newly limits SNAP to certain groups of lawfully present noncitizens, effective **February 1, 2026**. Many individuals remain eligible, including household members with U.S. citizenship and certain qualifying immigration statuses.

**Important:** Current recipients keep their benefits until their next recertification — there is nothing to do immediately at the time of this change taking effect. No repayment is required for benefits received before the law changed.

## Who Is Eligible (Starting February 1, 2026)

- U.S. Citizen
- U.S. National (e.g., born in American Samoa or Swains Island)
- Cuban and Haitian Entrant (CHE)
- Citizen of Micronesia, the Marshall Islands, or Palau
- Lawful Permanent Resident (LPR) who has met the 5-year waiting period, OR qualifies for an exemption from it:
  - Under 18 years old
  - Has 40 qualifying work quarters
  - Blind or disabled
  - Was lawfully residing in the U.S. and 65+ on August 22, 1996
  - Has a U.S. military connection
  - Previously admitted as Amerasian immigrant, adjusted to LPR status
  - Certain Hmong or Highland Laotian tribal members
  - Certain American Indians born abroad (INA 289 or Indian Self-Determination Act)
  - Certain other humanitarian noncitizen categories

## Who Is No Longer Eligible (Starting February 1, 2026)

Benefits will be terminated at application or next recertification for:
- Asylees
- Refugees
- Parolees (unless Cuban/Haitian Entrant)
- Individuals with deportation/removal withheld
- Conditional Entrants
- Survivors of Trafficking
- Battered Noncitizens
- Iraqi or Afghan Special Immigrant Visa (SIV) holders who are not LPRs
- Certain Afghan Nationals granted parole between July 31, 2021 and September 30, 2023
- Certain Ukrainian Nationals granted parole between February 24, 2022 and September 30, 2024

## Timing of the Change

A county worker reviews eligibility at the individual's **next recertification** — current benefits continue until then. Most households recertify every 6 months, though some have longer periods:
- 36-month certification: elderly-only households (65+) receiving SSI
- 12-month certification: elderly (60+) and/or disabled-only households with no earned income

## Status Changes

Individuals may become newly eligible if their status changes to: U.S. Citizen, U.S. National, CHE, Citizen of Micronesia/Marshall Islands/Palau, or LPR.

**Exemption retention for former humanitarian statuses:** Under FNS 227.06(B), individuals who previously held refugee, asylee, trafficking-victim, or other humanitarian status and have since adjusted to LPR status retain their exemption from the 5-year waiting period.

## Mixed-Status Households

SNAP eligibility is determined **per individual**, not per household. Some household members may remain eligible even if others do not. A notice is issued showing which individuals are approved/denied. Household benefit amount may be adjusted to reflect changed composition. Only the ineligible individual's portion is affected — other eligible members continue receiving benefits.

## Public Charge

As of March 9, 2021, SNAP is not considered a public charge program — receiving SNAP does not affect visa applications or family-based green card applications. (Note: proposed changes to public charge rules may affect this in the future; consult an immigration attorney with specific concerns.)

- Local food banks: feedingthecarolinas.org
- NC 2-1-1 (dial 2-1-1 or nc211.org) — 24/7, multiple languages, food pantry/meal program referrals
- NC WIC Program — for pregnant/postpartum/breastfeeding individuals, infants, and children up to age 5
