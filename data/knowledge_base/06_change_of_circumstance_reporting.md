# FNS/SNAP Change of Circumstance — Mandatory Reporting Requirements

**Document type:** Official program policy page
**Last verified:** July 2026

## What Changes Require Mandatory Reporting

Households must report the following changes, with specific deadlines:

### 1. Income Exceeding the Gross Income Limit

If FNS/SNAP monthly household income exceeds the maximum gross income limit for the household size (see FNS 360 — Determining Benefit Levels for current limits), the household must add all income sources: wages, WFFA, child support, SSI, unemployment benefits (UIB), Social Security, Veterans benefits, disability payments, income of new household members, etc.

**Deadline:** Must report by the 10th day of the month following the month income increases.

**Note for households with more than 10 members:** begin with $6,592 and add $557 for each additional member (reference figure; verify against current FNS 360 for exact current-year amount).

**Important nuance:** Any change from the original application information that occurs after the interview but before the notice of eligibility is received must be reported by the 10th of the month following the month the notice of eligibility is received. At initial application/before first certification, households are **not** required to report income changes until their next recertification.

### 2. ABAWD Work Hours Reduction

If the household includes an Able-Bodied Adult Without Dependents (ABAWD) and that person's work hours are reduced to under 80 hours per month, this must be reported.

**Deadline:** Must report by the 10th of the month following the month the work hours reduce.

See the ABAWD Work Requirements document for full exemption and work-hour details.

### 3. Lottery or Gambling Winnings

If the household receives lottery or gambling winnings over $4,250 (verify against current FNS 360 resource limit figures, which may differ), this must be reported.

**Deadline:** Must report by the 10th of the month following the month the winnings are received.

## Other Information That Can Be Reported (Non-Mandatory Updates)

Households may also proactively report changes to:
- Demographic information (date of birth, current address)
- Housing expenses
- Dependent care expenses
- Child support expenses
- Utility expenses
- Unearned income information
- Employment status (including self-employment)
- Current benefits
- Liquid resources
- Medical expenses
- Disability information
- Work non-participation information
- Veteran/military status
- Education status
- Volunteering/work training information
- Criminal history
- Immigration information
- Scholarships
- Pregnancy
- Adoption/foster care payment information

## General Reporting Guidance

Households should report all changes as they occur. Mandatory changes (income over limit, ABAWD hours reduction, large winnings) have strict deadlines as noted above. Other changes should still be reported promptly to keep the case accurate, even without a strict deadline.

## Documentation & Verification

If a reported change requires additional documentation, the household is notified and can submit via:
1. Online upload through ePASS (accepted formats: PDF, JPG, TIF, BMP; max file size 30MB), or
2. Mail, fax, or in-person delivery to the local county DSS office

Submitted documents are marked Accepted, Rejected, or Partially Rejected by the caseworker. Verification remains outstanding until requirements are met.
