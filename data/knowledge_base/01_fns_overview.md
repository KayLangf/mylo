# Food and Nutrition Services (Food Stamps) — Program Overview

## What FNS Is

Food and Nutrition Services (FNS, also known as Food Stamps) is a program that helps eligible people with limited incomes supplement their budgets so they can purchase food. The program is nationally known as the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP).

Benefits are issued on an EBT card, which works like a debit card, usable at stores that accept EBT/SNAP benefits.

## Latest Policy Update (as of page verification)

H.R. 1, signed into law on July 4, 2025, made significant changes to SNAP eligibility requirements. Two changes affect North Carolina SNAP participants:
- **Expanded work requirements** — effective December 1, 2025
- **New non-citizen eligibility rules** — effective February 1, 2026

A county's Department of Social Services screens applicants for these changes at their next recertification.

## Who May Qualify

You may be able to get benefits if you:
- Have no job or a low-paying job
- Receive Work First Family Assistance (WFFA), TANF, or SSI benefits (for people with disabilities)
- Are elderly or disabled, and low-income
- Are experiencing homelessness

To qualify, a household must meet rules about income, money/property ("resources"), and residency.

## Household Definition

You are a household of one (1) if you:
- Live alone
- Are experiencing homelessness
- Have roommates you do not cook and eat meals with

If you live with family or others and prepare/eat meals together, count each of those people in your household — including children and seniors in the home.

## Income and Resource Limits

For most people, FNS eligibility has limits on income and on money/property ("resources") a household can have. Applicants may still qualify if they work part-time, make a little money, or own a home or car.

After applying, caseworkers determine which limits apply and whether deductions (medical and/or shelter expenses) might help the household qualify.

## Other Eligibility Requirements

- Must be a North Carolina resident and a U.S. citizen or have certain legal status. Undocumented immigrants are not eligible.
- Must have a Social Security number or have applied for one.
- People convicted of certain drug-related felonies may not be eligible.
- College students are not eligible unless they meet certain work requirements or exceptions.
- Able-bodied adults without dependents may need to work or participate in a work program to keep receiving benefits.

## Benefit Disbursement Timing

Benefits are available on the same day each month, based on the last digit of the applicant's Social Security Number:

| SSN ends in | Benefits available after 6am on the: |
|---|---|
| 1 | 3rd |
| 2 | 5th |
| 3 | 7th |
| 4 | 9th |
| 5 | 11th |
| 6 | 13th |
| 7 | 15th |
| 8 | 17th |
| 9 | 19th |
| 0 | 21st |

If the applicant does not have a Social Security Number, benefits are available on the 3rd day of every month.

## What Can Be Purchased with FNS Benefits

**Allowed:**
- Breads, cereals, fruits, vegetables
- Meats, fish, poultry, dairy
- Bottled water, non-alcoholic beverages
- Seeds and plants that produce food

**Not allowed:**
- Beer, wine, liquor, cigarettes
- Nonfood items (pet food, soaps, household supplies)
- Vitamins and medicines
- Hot foods or foods already prepared
